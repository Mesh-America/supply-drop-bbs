# Code of conduct

This project is built and maintained by people for whom mesh radio
and BBSes are a hobby and a craft. We expect contributors and
participants to treat each other accordingly.

## The short version

**Be excellent to each other. If you can't, be elsewhere.**

## What that means in practice

- Argue with the work, not the person. "This design has a problem
  because X" is fair game; "you're an idiot for proposing this" is
  not.
- Disagree precisely. If you think something is wrong, say what
  specifically and why. Generic disapproval doesn't help anyone.
- Assume good intent until shown otherwise. Most disagreements come
  from missing context, not malice.
- When you're wrong, say so. When you're right, be gracious. Both
  matter for the long-term health of a project.
- Welcome new contributors with the same patience experienced
  contributors got when they started.
- Don't tolerate harassment, slurs, or systematic bad-faith
  participation. We will remove people who engage in those.

## What we won't tolerate

- Personal attacks, threats, or harassment of any kind.
- Slurs or demeaning language about race, ethnicity, religion,
  national origin, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation,
  disability, age, body, or any other protected characteristic.
- Doxxing or sharing private information about other participants
  without their consent.
- Sustained disruption of discussion: derailing, sealioning, bad-faith
  "just asking questions" tactics.
- Sexual attention or advances of any kind in project spaces.

## Enforcement

The project maintainers — currently Taedryn (TJ Downes) — are
responsible for enforcement. We will:

1. **Warn**, when the issue is minor or seems to come from a
   misunderstanding.
2. **Restrict** participation (lock a thread, block from a specific
   discussion) when warnings haven't worked or the issue is more
   serious.
3. **Ban** from the project's communication spaces when the behaviour
   is severe, repeated, or involves a clear violation of the
   "won't tolerate" list above.

We do not require multiple offences before taking action on serious
violations. The maintainer's judgement applies.

## Reporting

If you've been treated badly in a project space, or you've witnessed
someone else being treated badly, contact the maintainer privately
via a GitHub direct message or email (when one is published). Reports
will be treated confidentially.

Please include:

- What happened, with as much specificity as you're comfortable
  sharing
- Where (which issue, PR, channel)
- Whether you'd like a particular outcome
- Whether you want to remain anonymous in any follow-up

We will acknowledge receipt within 7 days.

## Scope

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces — issue tracker,
pull requests, code review, discussions, mailing lists if any, and
any project-affiliated chat. It also applies when individuals are
representing the project in public spaces, e.g. at a conference or in
a post that links to the project.

Behaviour outside project spaces is generally not our concern, with
the exception of severe public conduct that suggests an active threat
to project participants. We reserve the right to act on those at our
discretion.
